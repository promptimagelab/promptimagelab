How to Get the Fastest, Most Useful Response

Specific, detailed messages get specific, detailed responses. Vague messages lead to back-and-forth clarification that slows everything down. Here is what to include depending on your question type:

For prompt troubleshooting:

The exact prompt you used (copy-paste it), the AI platform and version, what you expected, what you got. Screenshots are welcome but not required.

For category suggestions:

The specific style or use case you want covered, any reference images or photographer names that define the aesthetic, and why you could not achieve it with existing prompts.

For partnership inquiries:

Describe the collaboration type, your platform and audience size, and what you see as the mutual value. We are selective about partnerships to maintain editorial independence.

For outdated content reports:

The specific guide URL, the outdated element (parameter, model version, feature name), and what the current correct information is if you know it.

What Happens After You Send a Message

1

Your message is read within 24 hours — not by a bot or auto-responder but by the person responsible for the content area your question relates to.

2

For prompt questions, we run your specific prompt on the AI platform you mentioned to reproduce and diagnose the problem. This is why we ask for exact prompt text and platform details.

3

For content corrections and category suggestions, we log them in our editorial backlog. You will receive a confirmation response and, for accepted category suggestions, a note when the guide is published.

4

We respond to all substantive messages within 24–48 hours on business days. During peak periods (new guide launches, model update cycles) response times may extend to 72 hours.
